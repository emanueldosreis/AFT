# Code Of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a respectful and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- using welcoming and inclusive language;
- being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences;
- accepting constructive criticism;
- focusing on what is best for the community;
- showing empathy toward other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language;
- sexualized language or imagery;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing others' private information without explicit permission;
- other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this code of conduct.

Maintainers may temporarily or permanently ban contributors for behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within project spaces and when an individual represents the project in public spaces, including using an official project email address, social media account, or event role.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers. All complaints should be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Maintainers are expected to respect the privacy and security of reporters. Enforcement actions may include warning, temporary restriction, permanent ban, or removal from project spaces.
